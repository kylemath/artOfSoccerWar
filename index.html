THE ART OF SOCCER WAR

100 CATCH PHRASES

FOR YOUTH CLUB COACHES AND PLAYERS

By Kyle Mathewson
Associate Professor, Department of Psychology
Faculty of Science, University of Alberta

Adapted from "The Art of War" by Sun Tzu
Based on the translation available at:
classics.mit.edu/Tzu/artwar.html

CHAPTER 1 - LAYING PLANS

  • 1.Plan for victory
    Good preparation matters more than just having strong players.
  • 2.Compare key factors
    Look at team spirit, coaching, game setting, plan, and discipline.
  • 3.Lead with values
    The team with better leadership and values usually wins.
  • 4.Know both teams
    A coach must know their team and the other team to win.
  • 5.Employ smart deception
    Trick the other team — act weak when strong, strong when weak.
  • 6.Confuse then press
    Mix up patterns, test them first, then force errors.
  • 7.Attack when ready
    Only attack when it makes sense.
  • 8.Foster team unity
    A well-led team with calm coaching plays like one body.
  • 9.Know to win
    If you know yourself and the other team, you'll win most games.

CHAPTER 2 - WAGING WAR

  • 10.Manage team energy
    Don't use all your energy at the start — rotate players smartly.
  • 11.Win swiftly
    Win fast when you can — long fights drain your team.
  • 12.Play efficiently
    Play sharp and don't waste moves.
  • 13.Start with pressure
    A fast start and early pressure can shake their confidence.
  • 14.Safeguard morale
    A tired or unhappy team doesn't play well.
  • 15.Coach strategically
    Good coaches plan, teach, and create teams that want to work hard.
  • 16.Think before running
    Smart play beats nonstop running.
  • 17.Break their will
    Steady pressure can make the other team give up.

CHAPTER 3 - ATTACK BY STRATAGEM

  • 18.Win without fighting
    The best win is disrupting their strategy before battle.
  • 19.Disrupt opponent unity
    Confuse them so they stop working as a team.
  • 20.Undermine confidence
    If you can't stop their teamwork, shake their belief.
  • 21.Seek alternative wins
    Try other ways before going into full battle.
  • 22.Outsmart stronger teams
    Even strong teams lose to smarter ones.
  • 23.Think ahead
    The best coaches win by thinking more clearly.
  • 24.Secure defense first
    Make your team solid before attacking — don't rush in.
  • 25.Choose right strategy
    There are different ways to win — choose what works.

CHAPTER 4 - TACTICAL DISPOSITIONS

  • 26.Organize before attacking
    Make sure your team is organized before trying to win.
  • 27.Win via positioning
    Victory comes from good positioning and timing, not just effort.
  • 28.Defend before attacking
    Be ready to defend before attacking.
  • 29.Choose favorable battles
    Don't fight unless the situation is good for you.
  • 30.Wait for moment
    Wait until you see the right moment to strike.
  • 31.Keep plays simple
    Great plays should look easy — focus on results, not praise.

CHAPTER 5 - ENERGY

  • 32.Move as one
    Great teams move as one, using passes and timing for big impact.
  • 33.Vary tempo
    Use both calm buildup and sudden attacks to confuse opponents.
  • 34.Favor simplicity
    Smart and simple plays beat wild and flashy ones.
  • 35.Control tempo
    Decide when to play fast or slow — don't let them control pace.
  • 36.Synchronize team
    When players move together, small actions create big results.
  • 37.Use player strengths
    Put each player where they do best.
  • 38.Maintain control
    You don't need chaos to win — control the field with sharp play.

CHAPTER 6 - WEAK POINTS AND STRONG

  • 39.Exploit weaknesses
    Go after open spaces and keep them guessing your approach.
  • 40.Flow around strength
    Flow around strong defenders into spaces they leave open.
  • 41.Feint then strike
    Pretend to go one way, then change direction to find gaps.
  • 42.Initiate action
    Don't wait to react — force the other team to adjust to you.
  • 43.Create space
    Pressure one side to open space on the other, then exploit it.
  • 44.Mask intentions
    Keep your real intentions hidden until it's time to act.
  • 45.Adapt continuously
    Avoid their strengths, adapt during the game as needed.
  • 46.Use speed advantage
    Fast and smart movements can beat bigger teams.

CHAPTER 7 - MANEUVERING

  • 47.Plan transitions
    Practice transitions so team shape changes are smooth.
  • 48.Avoid dead-ends
    Avoid tight spaces with no way out.
  • 49.Support teammates
    Stay connected — if the team doesn't work as one, even great players fail.
  • 50.Run purposefully
    Don't waste running — move with intention and timing.
  • 51.Manage fatigue
    Be smart with energy so players finish strong.
  • 52.Communicate clearly
    Use simple signals and give quick pre-game talks.
  • 53.Reset calmly
    When mistakes happen, don't panic — reset with confidence.
  • 54.Maintain morale
    Know what slows your team and maintain their happiness.

CHAPTER 8 - VARIATION IN TACTICS

  • 55.Adapt constantly
    Change your game plan based on conditions and opponents.
  • 56.Punish errors
    If the other team messes up, strike quickly.
  • 57.Stay unpredictable
    If you always play the same way, good teams figure it out.
  • 58.Evolve wisely
    Don't change just to be different — stick with what's working.
  • 59.Coach adaptively
    If a coach can't adapt, the team suffers.
  • 60.Handle surprises
    When things go wrong or opponents surprise you, adjust with control.

CHAPTER 9 - THE ARMY ON THE MARCH

  • 61.Read energy cues
    Watch how players and opponents act — energy levels tell you a lot.
  • 62.Stay compact
    Keep your team close enough to help each other quickly.
  • 63.Select smart positions
    Pick good spots on field, avoid corners and sidelines.
  • 64.Stay vigilant
    Watch for sudden changes in how the other team plays.
  • 65.Trust perception
    If something looks wrong, it probably is.
  • 66.Maintain shape
    Don't let your team get pulled apart — maintain formation and support.
  • 67.Balance formation
    Don't send everyone forward — keep some players back for safety.

CHAPTER 10 - TERRAIN

  • 68.Study field
    Learn about every field — size, surface, slopes, and conditions.
  • 69.Exploit space
    Understand which parts of the field help your team play better.
  • 70.Use field features
    Use features like width or slope to help your team.
  • 71.Dominate key zones
    Own the important spaces like the center of the field.
  • 72.Optimize spacing
    Spread enough to play but stay close enough to help each other.
  • 73.Guard both goals
    Pay attention to both goals — attacks come quickly.
  • 74.Adjust to venue
    Adjust your strategy based on where you're playing.

CHAPTER 11 - DIFFERENT GAME SITUATIONS

  • 75.Read flow
    Learn different situations — away games need focus, home games use familiarity.
  • 76.Seize key moments
    Some parts matter more — know when to push hard.
  • 77.Monitor energies
    Keep players connected and notice how tired or confident everyone looks.
  • 78.Protect leads
    When ahead, play smart; when behind, stay calm and look for chances.
  • 79.Build trust
    Let players know you believe in them and give clear directions.
  • 80.Guard secrets
    Don't share your game plan — surprise opponents and stay positive.
  • 81.Substitute wisely
    Make changes at the right time to help your team.
  • 82.Learn together
    Study other teams but remember unity beats individual talent.

CHAPTER 12 - USING INTENSITY

  • 83.Time intensity
    Know when to play with high or low intensity.
  • 84.Leverage conditions
    Take advantage of weather, sun, and wind when possible.
  • 85.Press wisely
    Press hard when it makes sense, back off when it doesn't.
  • 86.Use bursts
    Use short bursts instead of constant pressure — give recovery time.
  • 87.Control speed
    Match tempo to help your team — fast or slow.
  • 88.Train varied intensity
    Train with various intensities so players learn to adjust.
  • 89.Reserve strength
    Don't waste energy early if you'll need it later.
  • 90.Stay composed
    Keep motivation high but controlled — make smart choices about when to use energy.

CHAPTER 13 - GAME INTELLIGENCE

  • 91.Observe opponents
    Pay attention to how other teams warm up and play in different situations.
  • 92.Share insights
    Let players and coaches help each other by sharing what they see.
  • 93.Study habits
    Notice other teams' favorite moves and plays.
  • 94.Keep secrets
    Don't let other teams know your plans too early.
  • 95.Listen to players
    Ask smart questions and trust what your players tell you about the field.
  • 96.Analyze matches
    Have players study other matches to learn new ideas.
  • 97.Communicate quickly
    Share important information clearly and quickly during games.
  • 98.Debrief together
    After each game, talk about what worked and what didn't.
  • 99.Plan ahead
    Use what you learn to make better plans for next time.
  • 100.Value intelligence
    The team that sees and learns the most usually wins.
"The greatest victory is winning without fighting. The greatest coach teaches players to think, adapt, and work as one team."
